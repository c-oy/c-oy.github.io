About me

I am a Ph.D. student at the Department of Electronic and Information Engineering, Graduate School of Engineering, Tokyo University of Agriculture and Technology, Tokyo, Japan. I am currently a member of the Shimizu Laboratory.

I expect to obtain a Ph.D. in Engineering from the Tokyo University of Agriculture and Technology in March 2023.

My research deliverables are mainly in the fields of intelligent robotics and agricultural automation. Furthermore, I also focus on deep learning-based image processing.

Recently, I developed a novel mobile robot for crop monitoring in greenhouses.

  • ICARM2022

Because there were no similar robots for sale, I made the novel robot by hand.

It features a monitoring range that can cover all crops in greenhouses.

Using the novel robot, I proposed a tomato disease monitoring system that automatically monitors tomatoes and warns about diseased ones in greenhouses.

  • Agronomy

The animation below illustrates its working process.

In brief, the system automatically patrols the greenhouse, monitors the tomato plants, detects diseased tomatoes, and reports them to the user.

Besides the research, I am also very interested in DIY. Examples include a nixie tube&wireless charging clock, and a remote robot used for playing with a cat, etc. (Check)
